Perforated Prototype Design
01020 A/m²

Current density magnitude |J| (A/m²) beneath the anterior electrode. The solid design shows concentrated hotspots at both edges (edge effect). The perforated design redistributes current more uniformly, with K reduced from 2.82 to 2.76 at the 0.4 mm baseline hole diameter.

NSGA-II results across ~2,400 candidate designs. The Pareto front (highlighted) shows the best achievable trade-off between current non-uniformity K and peak tissue current density |J|. The solid baseline (×) sits at K = 2.56, peak |J| = 35.14 A/m². Best-K solution achieves K = 2.33 (−9.0%).
Non-uniformity K vs Back Pad Length
Heart Wall Mean |J| vs Back Pad Length
Edge Hotspot |J| (p95) vs Back Pad Length
Front pad fixed at 100 mm. K reaches a minimum of 6.13 at 140 mm. Heart wall mean |J| increases monotonically from 7.42 to 9.47 A/m², reflecting the larger current capture area. Edge hotspot decreases steadily from 3.37 to 2.58 A/m² as the back pad widens.
